A little context

Most teams don't have a lead problem.
They have a follow-up problem.

Leads come in. Then they sit — because someone is on a call, the CRM is a mess, or the handoff never happened. I've watched how much revenue quietly leaks out of that gap, and most of my work has been about closing it.

I'm a bilingual (English/Arabic) CRM and automation specialist who has spent 5+ years working remotely with U.S.-based real estate and sales teams. I design the systems that catch leads the moment they arrive, route them to the right person, and keep the follow-up running without anyone babysitting it.

That includes building an AI inbound call agent on the Claude API and Zapier that handled client follow-up and scheduling on its own — and the kind of CRM architecture, SOPs, and pipeline reporting that lets a small team operate like a much bigger one. I'm comfortable in the deal work too: contracts, escrow, disclosures, and closing paperwork.

If your operation is growing faster than your processes can keep up, that's usually where I'm most useful.
